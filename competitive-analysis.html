Executive Summary

Portsmouth's burger scene is entirely uncontested at the premium digital end. Our audit of 5 local competitors and 4 global inspiration brands reveals a critical gap: no Portsmouth burger brand owns a premium website, tells a compelling brand story, or provides a seamless online experience. The highest-scoring local competitor rated only 40/80 on our 8-point criteria — against a global benchmark of 70+.

A new chain (SoBe Burger, Smashburger brand, Sessions-backed) has confirmed a Portsmouth opening. Lush Bites has a narrow window to establish category dominance before that arrival.

Our recommendation: build a scroll-animated, premium website that tells the story of the smash, anchors Lush Bites as Portsmouth's home-grown quality-first brand, and converts every visitor into an order or a return visit. The competitive landscape has handed Lush Bites a gift. This report shows exactly how to take it.

UX Pattern Recommendations

📌

Pattern 01 — Persistent Order CTA

All three global brands keep their order button visible in the sticky nav at all times. Zero Portsmouth competitors do this. Lush Bites should have a persistent fire-coloured "Order Now" button visible at every scroll position on every page.

↔️

Pattern 02 — Horizontal Category Scroll

O'Tacos' horizontal pill tab bar (Build your own / Louisiana / Deals / O'Boss…) is the most mobile-friendly menu pattern in fast food. Apply to Lush Bites: Smash Burgers / Chicken / Loaded Fries / Drinks / Desserts — horizontal swipe on mobile, full bar on desktop.

🔥

Pattern 03 — USP Stat Blocks

Five Guys uses 3 animated stat tooltips as trust signals AND differentiation. For Lush Bites: "100% British Beef" · "Smashed to order — never pre-pressed" · "Zero frozen patties, ever". These answer the "is it actually good?" question before it's asked.

🚚

Pattern 04 — Delivery Partner Logo Strip

Five Guys and BK France prominently display Deliveroo, Just Eat, and Uber Eats logos. Not a single Portsmouth competitor does this. A row of 3 delivery logos tells visitors instantly: "you can get this wherever you are." Eliminates the #1 visitor objection without a word of copy.

📖

Pattern 05 — Origin Story Section

Every premium brand has a homepage story section. None of Portsmouth's burger brands tell any story. The Lush Bites narrative — why the smash, why Portsmouth, why 135 Queen Street — is a competitive weapon no chain can copy. It doesn't need to be long. Just real.

⭐

Pattern 06 — Embedded Social Proof

O'Tacos embeds Instagram with live like counts (2k–7k per post). Google review quotes embedded above the footer convert well. Show real reviews with names and star ratings — not just an aggregate score. Actual words beat numbers.

🗂️

Pattern 07 — Service Mode Visual Selector

Burger King France's three distinct service cards (King Drive / King Table / King Delivery) each with image + CTA immediately answer "how do I get this food?" For Lush Bites: Dine In · Click & Collect · Delivery as three visual cards with the right platform links behind each.

Animation Direction

✦

Scroll-Triggered Reveals

Every section fades/translates in on scroll via GSAP + ScrollTrigger. Hero text reveals staggered by word. Product cards cascade in on scroll. Smooth and cinematic — not bouncy or playful. 600–800ms duration, ease-out.

✦

Parallax Depth

Background food photography moves at 0.6× scroll speed. Foreground product cutouts move at 1.1×. Creates perceived 3D depth without performance overhead. Reference: O'Tacos hero carousel.

✦

Micro-interactions

Order button scales 1.04× on hover with fire-colour shadow. Menu cards lift 4px. Category tabs get fire underline on active. Nav links have 150ms colour transition. All easing: cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1).

✦

3D Hero Asset

Hero section built to receive a full-bleed 3D scroll-triggered burger reveal (produced separately). Placeholder clearly marked in code with exact dimensions. Cinematic quality — this is the stop-scroll moment.
