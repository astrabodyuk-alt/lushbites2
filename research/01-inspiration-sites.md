## Site 1: O'Tacos (o-tacos.com/fr)

### Brand Snapshot
- **Primary Color:** #E3DCCE (warm cream)
- **Secondary/Accent:** #FF7200 (vibrant orange)
- **Background:** #FFFFFF
- **Fonts:** Puffin Display Black (display/hero), Switzer (body)
- **Tone:** Playful, irreverent, youth-culture
- **Core Message:** "40,000 possible combinations" — mass customisation as identity

### Section Order (Top → Bottom)
1. **Sticky Nav** — Logo (left), Boss Club loyalty link, "I'm hungry" CTA button (right), language toggle
2. **Hero Carousel** — Full-screen rotating product features, each with product logo + tagline + "What is it?" CTA
3. **Product Category Scroll** — Horizontal pill tabs: Build Your Own / Louisiana / Deals / O'Boss / Cro'usty / FrerO' / O'Fresh / O'Brunch / O'Mini / O'Snack / Desserts / Drinks
4. **Boss-Sellers Grid** — 4-up card grid: product image + name + subtitle + "What is it?" link
5. **Location Map Section** — "Find your restaurant" with interactive map, 3 ordering modes (On site / Take away / Delivery)
6. **Ordering CTA** — "I'M ORDERING NOW" full-width section with order + click-and-collect options
7. **Boss Club Loyalty** — Rewards program feature with app download
8. **Instagram Social Feed** — Live Instagram grid (LightWidget), 12 posts shown with like counts (2k-7k likes)
9. **Footer** — 5-column: Products / Restaurants / The Family / Join Us / O'Tacos and Me

### Navigation Structure
- **Sticky header** throughout scroll
- Desktop: horizontal nav with mega-dropdown menus
- Mobile: hamburger with slide-in panel
- Two primary CTAs always visible: Boss Club (loyalty) + "I'm hungry" (order)

### Product Card Layout
- Image (hero cutout, product on transparent bg)
- Sub-brand logo above name
- One-line punchy tagline
- "What is it?" text CTA (never "Buy" or "Order" — discovery-first language)

### Primary CTA
- **Text:** "I'm hungry" / "J'ai faim"
- **Placement:** Top-right of nav + large mid-page section
- **Style:** Pill-shaped, warm cream (#F9F6EC), dark text — inviting not aggressive

### Menu Format
- Horizontal scrolling tab bar of product categories
- Each tab leads to a category page with full product grid
- No pricing shown on homepage — discovery first

### Trust Signals
- Instagram feed with raw engagement numbers (2k–7k likes)
- Boss Club loyalty program (rewards every order)
- "40,000 combinations" as a capability signal
- Click & Collect and delivery integration

### Key UX Lessons for Lush Bites
- Lead with product names and attitude, not category labels
- Use discovery language ("See more", "What is it?") over transactional CTAs
- Horizontal product category scroll works brilliantly on mobile
- Loyalty program shown above the fold = retention built-in
- Instagram proof embedded directly on homepage

## Site 2: Burger King France (burgerking.fr)

### Brand Snapshot
- **Primary:** #502314 (deep burger brown)
- **Secondary:** #FAF3E8 (warm paper)
- **Accent:** #D62300 (Burger King red)
- **Background:** #F5EBDC (warm sesame)
- **Fonts:** Flame Regular (custom heading), Flame Sans Regular (body)
- **Tone:** Bold, promotional, royal entitlement
- **Core Message:** "Soyez traité comme un King" — you deserve the best

### Section Order (Top → Bottom)
1. **Sticky Nav** — Logo + primary nav links + Kingdom loyalty + "Commander" CTA
2. **Hero — Full-Screen Promotions** — Animated hero banners (2 rotating), each linking to specific campaign
3. **News Grid** — 3-column card grid: "L'actualité Burger King" — current promotions
4. **Service Modes** — Drive-through / King Table / King Delivery / Click & Collect — visual service selector
5. **Selected For You** — Personalised product recommendations
6. **Classics Discovery** — Horizontal scroll of classic products with photos + names
7. **App Download CTA** — Full-width, dark background, prominent app store badges
8. **Footer** — 5 column with products / family / consumer service / legal / company

### Navigation Structure
- Sticky dark nav bar
- Secondary nav visible when scrolling: Notre carte / Coin famille / Bons plans / Engagements / Restaurants
- "Commander" (Order) always pinned in nav
- Kingdom (loyalty) program accessible from nav

### Product Card Layout
- Square image, product name below, no price on homepage
- Featured products get full-bleed hero treatment
- Seasonal/limited products highlighted in news grid

### Primary CTA
- **Text:** "Commander" (Order)
- **Placement:** Nav top-right + service selector section
- **Style:** Red (#D62300), 10px rounded, bold text

### Menu Format
- Separate /carte page with category tabs
- Homepage: curated selection + "Voir plus / Consulter la carte" link

### Trust Signals
- "Burger Kare" sustainability messaging in nav
- Family corner dedicated section
- Celebrity/influencer campaign imagery
- App store social proof (download counts implied)

### Key UX Lessons for Lush Bites
- Order button must always be visible in navigation
- Warm, paper-tone backgrounds (#F5EBDC type) feel premium and food-appropriate
- Service modes (dine-in/takeaway/delivery) as distinct visual cards is highly effective
- App download with social proof converts well
- Campaign/seasonal content in hero builds return visits

## Site 3: Five Guys UK (fiveguys.co.uk)

### Brand Snapshot
- **Primary:** #930B24 (deep red)
- **Accent:** #D21033 (brand red)
- **Background:** #F6F6F6 (light grey)
- **Font:** Public Sans (single font, weight variation)
- **Tone:** Bold, confident, authenticity-first
- **Core Message:** "Fresh Burgers, Fries & Milkshakes" — quality through simplicity

### Section Order (Top → Bottom)
1. **Sticky Nav** — Logo (left), full dropdown menu + "Order Now" (top right, red button)
2. **Hero** — Full-width promotional image, "Learn More" text CTA
3. **The Food Grid** — 8-category grid: Burgers / Dogs / Sandwiches / Breakfast / Fries / Shakes / Drinks / Toppings (image + label)
4. **USP Tooltip Section** — 3 rotating stats: "Extra Scoop of Fries Comes Standard" / "15 Free Toppings" / "Curious About Our Peanuts?" — each expandable
5. **Kids Meals CTA** — Isolated section, price anchor (from £7.50), "Order Now" + "See Menu"
6. **Find A Store** — Search bar + location map, "Search Locations"
7. **Gift Cards** — Full-width feature
8. **Delivery Partners** — Deliveroo / Just Eat / Uber Eats logos
9. **"Five Guys Unwrapped"** — Kitchen/ingredient transparency
10. **About Us Story** — Since 1986, family origin, image-rich
11. **We Love Our Crew** — Staff culture section
12. **Group Ordering CTA**
13. **Merchandise Shop CTA**
14. **Footer** — 4-col: Online Ordering / Customer Support / Nutrition / Download App / Social / Newsletter

### Navigation Structure
- Sticky white header
- Full dropdown: The Menu (7 subcategories) / About Our Food / Locations / Gift Cards / The Crew / Careers / Our Story
- "Order Now" always red and prominent in top-right
- Mobile: hamburger with search bar built-in

### Product Card Layout
- Square image, food-first photography (white/red bg)
- Category name in bold
- "Full Menu" anchor at end of grid
- Individual product pages with full ingredient transparency

### Primary CTA
- **Text:** "Order Now"
- **Placement:** Nav top-right (persistent), below hero, kids meal section, footer
- **Style:** Red (#D21033), 10px rounded, white text

### Menu Format
- 8-category image grid on homepage (visual menu)
- Dedicated /menu/ page with category tabs
- Individual product pages with customisation options (15 free toppings)
- Toppings as a separate menu category (unique, builds perceived value)

### Trust Signals
- "Since 1986" origin story
- "250,000+ ways to top a burger" (customisation as trust signal)
- Halal certification module
- Delivery partner logos (Deliveroo/Just Eat/Uber Eats)
- Newsletter sign-up
- Crew culture (employer brand builds consumer trust)

### Key UX Lessons for Lush Bites
- 8-category visual grid is extremely scannable — use this pattern for menu display
- USP section with expandable stats is a powerful differentiator communicator
- Delivery partner logos = instant trust signal (no customer has to think about "can I get this delivered?")
- Ingredient transparency section converts skeptical first-timers
- "Order Now" repeated 4+ times across page — repetition works
- Kids meal pricing anchor (from £X.XX) makes full menu feel accessible

## Site 4: The Hut (thehut.com)

> Note: DNS failure prevented scraping. Replaced with contextual analysis from similar premium e-commerce brands.

### UX Patterns Observed Across Premium D2C/E-commerce
- **Dark hero with product photography** on high-contrast backgrounds
- **Sticky add-to-cart or "order now"** visible on product pages always
- **Trust strip** below hero: reviews count / delivery promise / returns / security
- **Product grid** with hover state showing quick-buy
- **Social proof carousel** — actual review text with star ratings, not just aggregate score
- Abandoned cart / email capture typically integrated into hero or exit-intent

### Key UX Lessons for Lush Bites
- Trust strip (stars / reviews / delivery time) just below hero dramatically reduces bounce
- Review text beats review count alone — show 2-3 real quotes prominently
- Exit-intent email capture with discount converts well even on restaurant sites (first order 10% off)
