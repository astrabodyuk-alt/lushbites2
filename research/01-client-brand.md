## Competitive Positioning Gap

Lush Bites currently has **zero web presence** beyond delivery platforms. This is both the problem and the opportunity:
- No Google search visibility
- No brand storytelling surface
- No direct ordering capability (100% dependent on Uber Eats / Deliveroo taking 25-35% commission)
- No SEO footprint for local searches like "smash burger Portsmouth" or "halal burger Portsmouth"

**Opportunity:** First-mover advantage for a premium QSR website in the Portsmouth smash burger space. Messy Chef has a website; 7bone has a polished site; SMASHED and Mr SMASH are delivery-only. Lush Bites can immediately differentiate by having the most premium digital presence in the Portsmouth smash burger category.

## Recommended Brand Direction (to be refined in Phase 4)

Based on competitor gap analysis and menu positioning:

- **Primary palette:** Deep charcoal / near-black (`#1a1a1a`) for dark premium feel
- **Accent:** Fiery red-orange (`#e84c1e`) — matches the "Devil's Bite" and "spicy" brand thread
- **Warmth:** Warm cream (`#f5f0e8`) for backgrounds and cards — premium food photography contrast
- **Fonts (recommended):** `Bebas Neue` or `Playfair Display` for headings (bold, impactful) / `Inter` or `DM Sans` for body
- **Photography direction:** Dark, moody, close-up food shots with dramatic lighting — burger cross-sections, sauce drips, steam
