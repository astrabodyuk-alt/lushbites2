# Portsmouth Burger Competitor Analysis — Lush Bites Portsmouth

> Research Date: 2026-03-31  
> Scope: Top 5 smash burger / burger competitors in Portsmouth, UK  
> Sources: Firecrawl scrape, Google, TripAdvisor, Yelp, Reddit r/Portsmouth, Portsmouth.co.uk

---

## Market Overview

Portsmouth's burger scene is fragmented. The top-rated venues rely almost entirely on TripAdvisor and Google Maps for discovery — most have weak or non-existent standalone websites. This is a significant gap Lush Bites can exploit.

**Key insight:** No Portsmouth burger brand owns premium digital presence. The web is wide open.

## Competitor Profiles

---

### 1. 7Bone Burger Co
**URL:** 7bone.co.uk  
**Location:** 16 Guildhall Walk, Portsmouth PO1 2DD  
**Type:** Established burger chain (multi-city UK)  
**TripAdvisor:** Highly rated — "smashed patties superbly seasoned"

**Brand Identity**
- Colors: Hot pink (#EC008C), yellow (#FBD81C), black (#000000)
- Fonts: IAmTheCrayonMaster (custom display), Roboto (body)
- Tone: Extremely quirky/irreverent — "Burgers, Beers and Lols", "here for good times"
- Personality: Dive bar meets art school

**Site Structure**
- Homepage: Logo + animated GIFs + "Burgers, Beers and Lols" headline
- Menu: Main / Plant-based / Juniors / Drinks / Desserts
- Ordering: Click & Collect via 7bone.vmos.io
- Other: Shop (merch), Gallery, Loyalty, Careers

**Strengths**
- Strong local brand recognition
- Click & Collect available
- Loyalty program
- Multi-location legitimacy signal
- TripAdvisor presence is solid

**Weaknesses**
- Website has PHP/CraftCMS serialized query objects leaking as visible text (critical bug)
- Site is predominantly GIFs and audio — loads slowly, inaccessible
- Aesthetic is extremely niche — alienates mainstream audience
- No visible prices anywhere on website
- Menu pages lack food photography
- "Burgers, Beers and Lols" is not aspirational for premium audience
- No online ordering on-site — redirect to third-party

**Score: 5/10**

## Critical Gaps — What No One Is Doing in Portsmouth

1. **Premium standalone website** — not a single local competitor has one
2. **A brand narrative** — no one is telling the "why" of their smash burger
3. **High-quality food photography** — all rely on phone snaps or nothing
4. **Ingredient transparency** — "where does our beef come from?" is unanswered by everyone
5. **Smooth on-site ordering** — all use third-party redirects
6. **Local identity** — no one is owning "Portsmouth" as part of their brand story
7. **Trust strip with delivery partners** — none show Deliveroo/JustEat/Uber prominently

---

## Patterns of the Top 10%
(Observed across inspiration sites + best-in-class UK burger brands)

1. **Order CTA always visible** — persistent order button in sticky nav
2. **Warm, food-appropriate color palettes** — creams, ambers, deep reds — not clinical white
3. **Discovery-first language** — "Explore", "See more", not "Buy now"
4. **Customisation as hero story** — unique combinations = desire + personalization
5. **Delivery partner logos** — Deliveroo/Just Eat/Uber Eats shown = instant trust signal
6. **Ingredient transparency** — where it comes from builds premium positioning
7. **Loyalty program** — Boss Club (O'Tacos), Kingdom (BK) — retention built into brand

## Opportunity Summary for Lush Bites Portsmouth

Portsmouth's burger scene has a massive white space at the top:

- **No premium brand** — dominated by quirky independents and mid-tier chains
- **No storytelling** — nobody explains the craft of the smash
- **No premium digital presence** — the web is essentially uncontested
- **SoBe Burger incoming** — build now, own the market before the chain arrives
- **135 Queen Street location** — city centre, high footfall, ideal for dine-in + takeaway
- **Brand colors already ideal** — #e84c1e (fire orange-red) + #1a1a1a (near-black) + #f5f0e8 (warm cream) = distinctive, appetite-triggering, memorable
